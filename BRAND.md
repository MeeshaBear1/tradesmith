# Tradesmith — Creative Brief / Brand Bible

*Single source of truth for verbal + visual + content. Produced via the creative-suite Full Brand Sprint, 2026-06-06. Confidential.*

> **The one line that governs everything:** Tradesmith is a **well-made tool forged for the trade** — heavy-duty and dead-simple on the outside, quietly intelligent underneath. It treats the contractor like the professional they are, and makes a one-truck shop look like the biggest name in town. Every word, pixel, and post serves that.

---

## SECTION 1 — THE FOUNDATION

**Brand archetype:** **The Craftsman** (a grounded, blue-collar flavor of The Maker) — mastery, well-made tools, pride in the work. The name does the work: a *smith* forges tools for a trade, and Tradesmith forges the software the same way. Delivered in an **Everyman / Ally** register: peer-to-peer, on your side, never Silicon Valley talking down.

**Central tension:** **Built tough × Quietly smart.** The product feels like a premium hand tool — simple, fast, honest, built to take a beating — while running advanced AI takeoff and embedded fintech underneath. *Neither pole alone is the brand:* pure "rugged" is dumb software the trade has seen a hundred times; pure "AI/fintech" is the alienating SaaS the trade already hates.

**Positioning statement:** For the small contractor who'd rather be on the roof than in the office, **Tradesmith** is the trades operating system that turns an address into a paid job — because it was *forged by people who respect the trade*, not bolted together by enterprise software that doesn't.

**The one feeling:** **Respected.** Not "empowered," not "delighted." When a contractor uses Tradesmith, the software finally treats them like the pro they are — and makes them look like one to the homeowner. Protect this feeling above all.

---

## SECTION 2 — THE VOICE

**We are:** Straight-talking · Hard-wearing · Sharp (quietly expert)
**We are not:** Corporate / jargon-y · Cutesy or "disruptive" startup-y · Condescending (no Silicon Valley superiority)

**We write like** a sharp, respected foreman explaining the tool to another contractor over a tailgate — **not** like a SaaS landing page, and **not** like a beer-commercial caricature of "hard-working Americans." Respect is the line: we never perform blue-collar, and we never talk down.

**Tone axes:**
| Axis | Rating | Reads as |
|---|---|---|
| Formality | **3 / 10** | Plain, direct, jobsite-plain. Short sentences. Contractions. No jargon. |
| Confidence | **8 / 10** | Decisive, sure-footed. We know how the job works. No hedging, no boasting. |
| Warmth | **6 / 10** | On your side, dry jobsite humor allowed. Warm, not soft; never cuddly. |
| Pace | **7 / 10** | Fast, lean, gets to the point. The contractor is time-poor. |

**Voice do/don't:**
- ✅ "Quote it from the truck. Get paid before you pack up."
- ✅ "AI-assisted measurement. Confirm it on site — you're still the pro."
- ❌ "Leverage our AI-powered platform to streamline your workflow." (corporate jargon)
- ❌ "Roofing just got a glow-up ✨" (cutesy startup)
- ❌ "We know you work hard, so we built this for the little guy." (condescending)

---

## SECTION 3 — THE MESSAGE

**Master claim / brand promise:** **Quote it from the truck. Get paid before you pack up.**

**Pillar 1 — Forged for the trade.** Built around how the work actually happens: address in → takeoff out, your rate card, your brand. *Proof:* 16 trades on one estimate engine; AI satellite roof takeoff in under a minute; a contractor-editable rate card with regional factors.

**Pillar 2 — Look like the pro you are.** White-labeled, premium proposals that make a one-truck shop look like the biggest name in town — and let the homeowner pick their own tier. *Proof:* branded proposals; interactive Good/Better/Best; finished-project visualizations (roadmap).

**Pillar 3 — Get paid, not chased.** Deposit in the bank the day you quote; finance the homeowner so price isn't the "no." *Proof:* Stripe same-day deposit; "finance this roof"; no more 45-day check chase.

*(Credibility undercurrent across all three — **Straight with you:** honest AI, fair pricing, no enterprise lock-in or year-long contract.)*

**Taglines**
- **Selected (recommended): "Forged for the field."** — carries the Craftsman archetype and the tension in three words; distinctly *not* enterprise SaaS.
- Functional descriptor (pairs with it): **"The trades operating system."**
- Provocation (campaign): **"Stop quoting at the kitchen table at 9pm."**
- Aspirational: **"Built like the work."**

## SECTION 4 — THE LOOK

**Visual register:** *A well-made tool, not an app.* The world of a premium hand tool, a foreman's field notebook, and a shop drawing — brought into a crisp, modern, high-contrast UI. Substantial, rectilinear, materially honest. The concept that ties the palette to the name: **cold steel and the forge fire** — a smith works cool grey steel, heated by the orange of the forge.

**Typography**
- **Display / wordmark / big numbers — Archivo** (700–800, Expanded width for the wordmark). Sturdy industrial-signage grotesque; reads stamped-into-steel. *Serves Formality 3 (no serif) + the Craftsman "stamped maker's mark."*
- **Body / UI — Hanken Grotesk** (400–700). Clean, sturdy, serious-but-human; bulletproof legibility for a tool used 8×/day. Tabular figures (`tnum`) on all money so columns never reflow. *Serves Warmth 6 + Pace 7.*
- **Spec / measurements — JetBrains Mono** (used sparingly: measurement readouts, SKU/spec chips, "stamped" uppercase labels). The "measured / quietly smart" pole made visible. *Deliberately not IBM Plex Mono — that's [Atlas].*

**Color palette — "Cold Steel & Forge Fire"**
| Token | Hex | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Forge Orange** (`--brand`) | `#EA4E1C` | The spark. Primary action, the price reveal, "live/hot." Used **sparingly** — discipline is the brand. |
| **Forged Graphite** (`--ink`) | `#1A1D22` | Cool charcoal. Text + dark surfaces. *Replaces the warm-stone ink.* |
| **Cold Concrete** (`--paper`) | `#F0F2F4` | Cool light-grey page ground. **The key break from the Plainsight warm-cream family.** |
| **Blueprint Steel** (`--trust`) | `#34566B` | Muted steel-blue. Warranty / insured / verified seals, secondary CTA. Credibility without leaning on orange. |
| Surface / Sunken | `#FFFFFF` / `#E9ECEF` | Cards / line-item wells. |
| Muted (slate) | `#677079` | Cool slate secondary text. *Replaces warm stone.* |

**Photography direction:** Real jobsites, real crews, real hands and tools — natural light, a little grit. The torn-off roof, the ladder, the truck, the finished roof at golden hour. **Never** stock "smiling contractor with a clipboard," never abstract tech imagery (no circuit boards, glowing AI orbs, hexagons). On the homeowner proposal, the hero is *their* house — the "after."

**Texture / materiality:** Subtle, precise — **a hairline blueprint/graph-paper grid** on heroes and empty states (never a dashed gray box), warm-neutral elevation shadows on the cool base, stamped uppercase spec labels, tabular numerals. Crafted, not lo-fi; not glossy.

**Anti-references (do NOT look like):**
- Generic SaaS — purple gradients, 3D blobs, friendly rounded illustrations, Inter-default landing pages.
- Cutesy/disruptive startup — emoji icons (kill the 🏠), neon, mascots, bouncy rounded everything.
- Condescending tech — abstract "AI" circuitry/orbs.
- Sibling collisions: **not** [Tomaso] navy + amber industrial deck; **not** the [Plainsight] warm-cream + teal + terracotta + Fraunces serif; **not** [Atlas] dark-cobalt enterprise spine.

**The unexpected move:** A **maker's mark.** The wordmark is struck like a smith's stamp in steel (heavy Archivo Expanded, an anvil/T monogram), and it sits *beside* the contractor's own logo on every proposal — Tradesmith forged the tool; the contractor signs the work. Forge-orange "heat" appears **only** at the moments of action, so orange always means *act / live / hot* and the page stays cool steel.

## SECTION 5 — THE INTEGRATION PROOF

**Voice → visual translation**
- "We are **straight-talking**" → high-contrast graphite-on-concrete, plain grotesque type, no decorative flourish.
- "We are **hard-wearing**" → rectilinear forged forms, substantial weights, material texture (blueprint grid, stamped labels).
- "We are **sharp / quietly smart**" → precise tabular numerals + mono spec chips + the disciplined single forge-orange accent.

**Central tension expressed in both:** *Built tough × Quietly smart* lives in the palette itself — **cold forged steel** (tough) + the **forge fire** used only with precision (smart, disciplined). In copy: blunt jobsite plain-talk *about* genuinely advanced AI/fintech, never showing off.

**Archetype consistency:** Visual (forged materials, stamped mark, tool-not-app) and verbal (smith, forged, respect the trade, built like the work) both serve **The Craftsman**. No divergence.

## SECTION 6 — THE CONTENT SYSTEM

**Primary channel:** **Instagram + Facebook (jobsite reels / before-after / contractor groups)** — where roofers and trades actually congregate and where visual proof wins. **Owned channel: email** (nurture + product). **YouTube** for long-form how-to authority later. *Avoid the B2B reflex:* LinkedIn is not where roofers are.

**Content pillars (each traces to a key message):**
- **The Tool** ← *Forged for the trade.* Short product walkthroughs, "quote a roof from the truck," rate-card setup. **Weekly.**
- **The Look** ← *Look like the pro.* Before/after, the proposal as a sales weapon, win stories. **Weekly.**
- **The Money** ← *Get paid, not chased.* Same-day deposits, financing the homeowner, the business-of-the-trade. **Bi-weekly.**

**Register:** Field-credible and spec-precise — proof-based, plain-spoken, jobsite-real. Show the work. Email is a touch more personal (foreman-to-foreman); social is fast and visual.

**Content anti-pattern (off-brand):** generic "10 marketing tips for contractors" listicles, AI-hype posts, anything that talks down to the trade or performs blue-collar for an outside audience.

**First 4 weeks (real titles):**
- *Wk1 — The Tool:* "Measure a roof from your truck in 60 seconds (and what the AI gets wrong on purpose)."
- *Wk1 — The Look:* "Same quote, two proposals: the napkin vs. the Tradesmith page. Which one wins the job?"
- *Wk2 — The Money:* "You finished the roof three weeks ago. Where's your check? Here's how same-day deposit works."
- *Wk2 — The Tool:* "Set your rate card once. Now every quote is priced *your* way — not a template's."
- *Wk3 — The Look:* "Before/after: how showing the homeowner their finished roof closed a $22k re-roof."
- *Wk3 — The Tool:* "Not just roofing — quoting siding, gutters, and windows on the same screen."
- *Wk4 — The Money:* "Price isn't the 'no.' How financing turned a $24k stall into a signed deposit."
- *Wk4 — The Look:* "Your logo, your color, your proposal. Tradesmith just forged the tool."

## SECTION 7 — THE PROOF OF COHERENCE

- **Does the copy voice match the visual register?** **Yes.** Plain, sturdy, confident copy ↔ forged-steel, high-contrast, stamped visual. Both are "a well-made tool, no BS."
- **Does the content system serve the positioning?** **Yes.** Every pillar maps 1:1 to a message; all three prove "turns an address into a paid job, forged by people who respect the trade."
- **Would someone who only saw the design recognize the brand in the copy?** **Yes.** Cold steel + disciplined forge-orange + stamped grotesque could only carry plain, expert, no-nonsense words.
- **The single most important thing to protect:** **Respect for the trade** — in the voice (never condescend) and in the discipline of the forge-orange spark (never let "tough" become dumb, or "smart" become show-off). The brand holds **both** poles; neither alone is Tradesmith.
